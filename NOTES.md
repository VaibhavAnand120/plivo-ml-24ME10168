# NOTES

The model predicts end-of-turn probability using handcrafted prosodic and acoustic features extracted only from audio preceding each pause. It relies primarily on energy decay, pitch movement, voiced duration, voiced fraction, spectral ratio, and zero-crossing rate. Speaker-normalized pitch helps reduce variability across different speakers. A HistGradientBoostingClassifier combines these features to estimate the probability of an end of turn. The model performs well on clear declarative speech with natural turn endings. It is less reliable on hesitations, filled pauses, and expressive speech where prosodic cues resemble true turn endings. Very short utterances and noisy recordings also remain challenging. With one more day, I would perform feature importance analysis, hyperparameter optimization, probability calibration, and error analysis to further reduce interruption rate while maintaining low response delay.
